External Factors Affecting Water Pollution

Industrial Waste

Factories release untreated or partially treated wastewater containing harmful chemicals such as solvents, oils, heavy metals, and toxic elements. These pollutants cause immense environmental devastation (EPA, Britannica).

Industrial activities also contribute to thermal pollution, where heated water from industrial processes is discharged into rivers, disrupting aquatic ecosystems and harming marine life.

Sewage and Wastewater

Poorly managed sewage systems in both urban and rural areas often discharge untreated or partially treated wastewater into water bodies. This introduces pathogens, nutrients, and chemicals, leading to waterborne diseases.

Agricultural Runoff

Agricultural fertilizers contain nitrates and phosphates, which leach into water systems through runoff. Pesticides, herbicides, and other chemicals from farming further contaminate rivers and lakes, causing nutrient pollution, algal blooms, and the degradation of water quality (EPA, Fair Planet).

Plastic Pollution

Improper waste disposal leads to significant amounts of plastic waste entering rivers, lakes, and oceans. Over time, plastics break down into microplastics, which harm aquatic life and can enter the food chain, posing health risks.

Mining Activities

Mining operations release toxic substances such as cyanide, mercury, and sulfuric acid into nearby water bodies. These chemicals have long-term detrimental effects on aquatic life and contaminate surrounding water sources (EPA).

Climate Change

Climate change is exacerbating water pollution by altering temperature and precipitation patterns. Warmer waters contribute to the growth of toxic algal blooms, which deplete oxygen levels and release harmful toxins into water bodies.

Urbanization

Increased urbanization leads to more impervious surfaces such as roads and buildings, which increase stormwater runoff. This runoff carries pollutants like oils, heavy metals, and chemicals from urban streets into nearby water sources.
