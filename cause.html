Causes of Water Pollution in Penang

Industrial Effluents

Wastes from factories contain heavy metals and other pollutants, which contaminate the water. The sea off Teluk Bahang has been reported to be polluted with heavy metals.

Source: CEMACS USM

Sewage and Domestic Waste

The contribution of untreated or partially treated sewage has been reported to deteriorate water quality, particularly in areas near Sungai Pinang.

Source: FOE Malaysia

Urban Runoff

Stormwater runoff from urban areas carries pollutants like gasoline, motor oil, heavy metals, fertilizers, and pesticides into water bodies. This leads to increased sedimentation and nutrient loading, further deteriorating water quality.

Marine Activities

Accidental spills and deliberate dumping of waste from vessels contribute pollutants that destroy coral and seagrass habitats, threatening coastal ecosystems.

Source: POGO Ocean

Agricultural Runoff

Fertilizers and pesticides from agricultural activities wash into rivers and coastal waters, leading to nutrient pollution and harmful algal blooms.

Solid Waste Disposal

Improper disposal of solid waste, including plastics and other debris, results in the accumulation of trash in waterways, affecting both water quality and marine life.

Water Pollution Issues in Penang

Penang faces serious water pollution problems, affecting both rivers and coastal waters.

River Pollution

The Pinang River, flowing through George Town, has long been considered one of the most polluted rivers in Penang. From 2006 to 2016, its Water Quality Index (WQI) ranged between Class III (requiring extensive treatment) and Class IV (only suitable for irrigation). While rehabilitation efforts have been made, its water quality has not significantly improved.

Coastal Pollution

In April 2019, almost 50,000 fish were found dead in Teluk Bahang due to a sudden decline in water quality. Heavy metals such as nickel, lead, and cadmium were detected, but the exact source of contamination remains unclear. Illegal dumping or dredging is suspected.

Source: POGO

Major Sources of Pollution

  • Untreated domestic sewage
  • Industrial effluents
  • Wastes from animal husbandry (e.g., swine farming in Kampung Selamat)

Source: PUBMED

The Need for Action

These pollution issues highlight the urgent need for integrated water management, pollution control, and environmental conservation efforts to protect aquatic ecosystems and public health in Penang.
