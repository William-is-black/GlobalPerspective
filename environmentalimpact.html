Environmental Impact

Water Pollution in Penang

Water pollution in Penang poses a serious threat to rivers, marine ecosystems, and coastal waters. Polluted rivers like Sungai Pinang, affected by industrial waste, untreated sewage, and litter, have led to oxygen depletion, habitat destruction, and mass fish kills.

Plastic pollution is a major concern, endangering marine species due to ingestion and entanglement. Nearby coral reefs are at risk due to sediment runoff from farming and construction, which threatens biodiversity and disrupts tourism.

Additionally, pollution weakens natural buffers like wetlands and mangrove forests, which act as barriers against erosion and floods. The destruction of these ecosystems threatens biodiversity, disrupts fisheries, and impacts tourism, affecting the livelihoods of local communities.

Global Environmental Impacts of Water Pollution

Water pollution is a critical global threat to ecosystems and biodiversity. Eutrophication, caused by excessive nutrients from fertilizers, leads to algal blooms, oxygen depletion, and mass die-offs of aquatic life.

The presence of plastics and microplastics in oceans affects marine animals and enters the food chain, posing health risks to humans. Industrial chemicals and heavy metals contaminate water bodies, causing long-term damage to freshwater and marine ecosystems.

Additionally, water pollution disrupts natural carbon sinks such as mangroves and wetlands, reducing their ability to absorb carbon dioxide and increasing climate-related vulnerabilities. The loss of biodiversity and healthy ecosystems affects food security, increases the risk of natural disasters, and diminishes cultural and recreational values associated with water bodies.

The Need for Action

Combating water pollution requires global cooperation through strict legislation, improved waste management, and sustainable practices. Governments, industries, and communities must work together to protect water resources for future generations.
