Effects of External Factors on Water Pollution

Industrial Discharge

Industries, especially those near coastal areas, release enormous quantities of waste, including plastics and toxic chemicals, into water bodies. These pollutants accumulate over time, leading to long-term pollution.

Source: United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP)

Agricultural Runoff

Fertilizers, pesticides, and herbicides used in agriculture wash into rivers and oceans during rainfall, contaminating water sources. Plastics used in farming, such as plastic films and containers, also contribute to water pollution.

Source: United States Environmental Protection Agency (EPA)

Urban Development

Urbanization increases impervious surfaces such as concrete and asphalt, which prevent water from soaking into the ground. As a result, rainwater carrying plastics and waste flows directly into rivers, lakes, and oceans through storm drains.

Source: United States Geological Survey (USGS)

Shipping and Maritime Activities

Ships, particularly in busy maritime routes, frequently discharge plastic waste, packaging materials, and lost cargo into the ocean. Commercial fishing operations also contribute to marine pollution through the loss of nets, fishing lines, and plastic equipment.

Source: International Maritime Organization (IMO)
