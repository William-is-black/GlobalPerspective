Introduction

Water pollution is a critical environmental issue that affects every corner of the globe. The causes and severity of pollution differ by region, influenced by industrial practices, population density, and economic conditions.

Japan: A Model for Water Management

Japan is recognized for its effective water pollution management. Through strict laws like the Water Pollution Control Act, advanced technology, and public engagement, the country maintains clean water resources and serves as a global example.

  • Advanced wastewater treatment plants
  • Stormwater management systems
  • Polluted water body restoration (e.g., Tokyo Bay)

India: Facing Water Pollution Challenges

In India, nearly 70% of surface water is contaminated due to untreated sewage, industrial waste, and agricultural runoff. Efforts like *Namami Gange* aim to address this, but significant challenges remain in enforcement and infrastructure.

  • High levels of untreated sewage and industrial waste
  • Laws like the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act
  • Infrastructure and technological gaps
