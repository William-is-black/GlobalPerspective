Understanding Water Pollution

Water pollution is a global problem with local impacts, affecting communities' health, industries, and ecosystems. Let's explore the causes, effects, and efforts toward solving it.

Our Goals

  • Educating on local water pollution issues.
  • Comparing water pollution in different regions.
  • Understanding the environmental impact of polluted waters.

External Factors Affecting Water Pollution

Industrial Waste

Factories release untreated or partially treated wastewater containing harmful chemicals like solvents, oils, and heavy metals. These pollutants cause immense environmental devastation (EPA, Britannica).

Industrial activities also contribute to thermal pollution, where heated water from industrial processes is released into rivers, affecting aquatic life and ecosystems.

Sewage and Wastewater

Poorly managed sewage systems discharge untreated or partially treated wastewater into water bodies, introducing pathogens and chemicals. This leads to waterborne diseases.

Agricultural Runoff

Fertilizers contain nitrates and phosphates that leach into water systems, causing nutrient pollution, algal blooms, and water degradation. Pesticides and herbicides further contaminate rivers and lakes (EPA, Fair Planet).

Plastic Waste

Improper waste disposal allows plastic waste to reach rivers, lakes, and oceans. Over time, plastics break down into microplastics, which harm aquatic life and enter the food chain.

Mining Activities

Mining releases toxic substances such as cyanide, mercury, and sulfuric acid into nearby water bodies. These chemicals have long-term effects on aquatic ecosystems and water contamination (EPA).

Climate Change

Climate change worsens water pollution by altering temperature and precipitation patterns. Warmer waters promote toxic algal blooms, which deplete oxygen and release toxins.

Urbanization

Urban development increases impervious surfaces like roads and buildings, leading to stormwater runoff. This runoff carries pollutants such as oils, heavy metals, and chemicals into local water bodies.
